Program Design Master of Worldview Studies in Education

A Flexible Path to Deeper Practice

Two core courses, a rich catalogue of electives, and a capstone pathway, all designed so you can complete your degree in about 2 years while working full-time.

Curriculum Overview

Shaped by Faith, Grounded in Practice

The MWS-E draws from ICS's rich catalogue of education courses and its decades-long commitment to integrally Christian scholarship. This program is designed to give working educators (teachers and administrators) the space to reflect, grow, and sharpen their practice in a community of peers.

Every course in the MWS-E is:

  • Biblically informed and focused on the love embodied by Christ
  • Deeply contextualized and relevant for educators
  • Aligned with your own professional passion and practice
  • Pursuing wisdom through sound scholarly methods

Program Snapshot

  • Core Courses 2 Courses
  • Elective Courses 4 OR 5 Courses
  • Capstone Action Project and Paper OR Research Paper
  • Delivery Fully Online
  • Format Part-Time Only
  • Timeline ~2 Years
  • Tuition $1,500 / Course
  • Bursary $500 / Semester

Core Courses

The Foundation of Your MWS-E

Every MWS-E student takes these two foundational courses. They establish the biblical and philosophical grounding that shapes the rest of your journey.

Biblical Foundations | Nik Ansell

Explore the biblical story as the grounding narrative for Christian educational practice and worldview formation. This course invites you to read Scripture afresh and discover how its overarching themes shape what it means to teach and lead.

Core 3 Credits Fall

What Is Christian About Christian Education? | Neal DeRoo

Engage Reformational philosophy to develop a deeply Christian understanding of the educational task. Explore how worldview, faith, and the rich ICS tradition of integral scholarship inform what happens in the classroom and beyond.

Core 3 Credits Summer

Capstone Pathways

Two Ways to Complete Your Degree

The MWS-E offers flexibility in how you finish. Choose the capstone that best fits your professional goals and learning style.

Practitioner Pathway

6 Courses + Action Project + Summative Paper

Ideal for educators who want to apply their learning directly in their school context. Carry out a hands-on Action Project and write a summative paper reflecting on the experience.

  • 2 Core + 4 Elective courses
  • Action Project in your own school setting
  • Summative paper reflecting on the project
6 Courses Action Project Summative Paper
Research Pathway

7 Courses + Research Paper

Suited for those drawn to deeper scholarly inquiry. Complete an additional elective and write a major research paper exploring a topic at the intersection of faith, education, and practice.

  • 2 Core + 5 Elective courses
  • Major research paper on a topic you choose
  • Deeper engagement with scholarly methods
7 Courses Major Research Paper

*Students may extend their timeline depending on course load and scheduling needs. Study at a pace that fits your life.

Elective Courses

Shape Your Own Learning

Choose 4–5 electives from ICS's education catalogue. Courses rotate on a regular schedule and each is delivered online with asynchronous work and biweekly Zoom sessions.

Transformative Teaching: The Practice of Christ-Centred Education | Edith van der Boom

Examine what it means to teach in ways that invite genuine transformation in learners and in yourself. Explore the intersection of pedagogy, faith, and formative practice.

Elective 3 Credits Winter

The Craft of Reflective Practice | Gideon Strauss

Develop the habits and skills of a reflective practitioner who can examine, articulate, and improve their own professional work, moving from experience to insight.

Elective 3 Credits Fall

Cultivating Learning Communities of Belonging | Edith van der Boom

Learn to foster inclusive, just, and welcoming school communities where every student is known and valued. Explore what belonging looks like in a Christian learning environment.

Elective 3 Credits Fall

How to Coach a Strong Team | Gideon Strauss

Build the leadership capacities needed to mentor, coach, and develop strong collaborative teams in your school. Learn to lead with wisdom and care.

Elective 3 Credits Winter

Called to Teach: From Formation to Transformation | Edith van der Boom

Reflect on teaching as a vocation: a calling rooted in faith, identity, and purpose. Reconnect with the deeper story of why you became an educator in the first place.

Elective 3 Credits Summer

Lead From Where You Are | Gideon Strauss

Whether you lead a classroom, a department, or a school, this course equips you to exercise leadership with integrity and vision from whatever position you hold.

Elective 3 Credits Summer

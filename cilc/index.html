CILC

Christian Instructional
Leadership Certificate

A four-course graduate certificate for classroom teachers seeking to deepen their faith-informed teaching practice.

The Christian Instructional Leadership Certificate (CILC) is an ICS-issued professional credential designed for K–12 classroom teachers who desire to deepen their Christian worldview in the context of their teaching practice.

Through four graduate-level courses, the CILC equips educators to integrate faith and practice, meet the needs of all learners, and cultivate a growth mindset grounded in a biblical vision of education. Each course also counts as graduate credit toward the MA-EL or MWS-E.

Required Courses

Four Courses to Complete the CILC

Complete all four required courses below. One alternate is available as noted.

Required

Called to Teach: From Formation to Transformation

Explore your vocation as an educator, reflecting on teaching practice in the context of faith and spiritual disciplines. An inner journey designed to inspire refreshment and renewal in your work within Christian education.

CILC Required CSTC Area 2 & 3 Summer Term
Required

Deeper Learning: From Wonder to Inquiry to Practice

Explore learning as a journey from wonder to inquiry to action. Develop Deeper Learning within the context of celebrating the learner as image-bearer, mindful learning design, and a responsiveness to culture.

CILC Required Winter Term
Required

Cultivating Learning Communities of Belonging

Explore school and classroom cultures, including social and cultural contexts, racial justice, Indigenous perspectives, human sexuality, and restorative practices — and how these topics impact and form school and classroom cultures.

CILC & CSAC Required CSTC Area 3 Fall Term
Required

Lead From Where You Are

Explore positional and contributory leadership, algorithmic and heuristic practices, and the work of motivating a group of people to shape what they share — making beneficial change happen and ensuring needed maintenance.

CILC & CSAC Required CSTC Area 2 Summer Term
Alternate

Transformative Teaching: The Practice of Christ-Centred Education

An alternate for Deeper Learning. Consider your role as a Christian educator called to be a transformer of society and culture. Examines constructivism through the lens of Scripture and explores Christian practices within teaching.

Can replace Deeper Learning CSTC Area 2

Note: Transformative Teaching: The Practice of Christ-Centred Education may be taken in place of Deeper Learning. All CILC courses also count as graduate credit toward the MA-EL or MWS-E.

Who Is It For?

Designed for Classroom Teachers

The CILC is designed for K–12 educators who are committed to growing as instructional leaders in their schools and classrooms.

Desire to deepen your Christian worldview in the context of teaching
Committed to integrating faith and instructional practice
Passionate about meeting the needs of all learners
Growth mindset and a desire to practice student-led learning

How It Works

Earning Your CILC

The CILC is administered and issued directly by ICS. Complete four courses and receive your certificate.

Step 1

Register for CILC courses through the ICS Academic Registrar.

Step 2

Complete all four required courses (one alternate is available).

Step 3

Receive your CILC from ICS, and continue toward a master's degree if you choose.

Dr. Edith van der Boom

Associate Professor of Philosophy of Education and Practice of Pedagogy

Director, MA-EL & MWS-E Programs

PhD (University of Toronto) · MEd · BA

Edith's Vision
My philosophy of education is rooted in the belief that each student is uniquely created by God and that education should celebrate both diversity and community. I believe that Christian schools need to be places of inclusive education where solid foundations are provided for all students, both spiritually and academically.

Courses Taught

  • Called to Teach: From Formation to Transformation
  • Christian Deeper Learning
  • Cultivating Learning Communities of Belonging
  • Transformative Teaching: The Practice of Christ-Centred Education

Research Interests

  • Inclusive Education
  • Inquiry-Based Learning
  • Deeper Learning
  • Differentiated Instruction
  • Decolonizing Education

Selected Publications

  • van der Boom, E. H. (2024). Participating in God's redemptive work: A cyclical model for learning and assessment. Int. Journal of Christianity & Education, 28(1), 341–352.
  • van der Boom, E. H., & Jang, E. E. (2018). The effects of holistic diagnostic feedback intervention on improving struggling readers' reading skills. Journal of Teaching and Learning, 12(2), 54–69.

Dr. Gideon Strauss

Associate Professor of Leadership and Worldview Studies

ICS Academic Dean

PhD (University of the Orange Free State, South Africa) · MA · BA

Gideon's Vision
I think about the stories we humans tell to account for what we do in terms of what we want. I teach, do research, and serve the wider community in, and at the intersection of, the fields of leadership and worldview studies, with an interdisciplinary approach that attends, specifically, to phenomenological and ethnographic methods of inquiry.

Dr. Neal DeRoo

Professor of Philosophy

PhD (Boston College) · MA (Institute for Christian Studies) · BA (Calvin College)

Neal's Vision
My work tries to account for how spirituality is constantly at work in constituting our experience and the implications that has for how we live our lives. I speak to academic, popular, and professional audiences, including doing professional development work with Christian day school teachers, showing that the deep-seated spiritual forces shaping our experience help us think about faith as something that impacts our everyday life together.

Courses Taught

  • What Is Christian About Christian Education?

Research Interests

  • Phenomenology
  • Philosophy of Religion
  • Spirituality & Experience
  • Christian Education

Selected Publications

  • DeRoo, N. (2026). Material Spirituality: A Transcendental Phenomenology of Religion. Bloomsbury Academic.
  • DeRoo, N. (2022). The Political Logic of Experience: Expression in Phenomenology. Fordham University Press.
  • DeRoo, N. (2013). Futurity in Phenomenology: Promise and Method in Husserl, Levinas, and Derrida. Fordham University Press.

Dr. Nik Ansell

Associate Professor of Theology

PhD (VU University, Amsterdam) · MPhil (Institute for Christian Studies) · BA (University of Bristol)

Nik's Vision
Theology includes reflection on key meta-theological issues that frame the discipline: how the sacred is related to life as a whole, as the hope or heartbeat of the universe, as the revelation of God, self, world, and other. My teaching and research focus on systematic and biblical theology, notably Christology, eschatology, Old Testament wisdom thinking, and the theology of gender.

Courses Taught

  • Biblical Foundations: Narrative, Wisdom, and the Art of Reading Scripture

Research Interests

  • Christology & Eschatology
  • Old Testament Wisdom Thinking
  • Theology of Gender
  • Phenomenology of Revelation
  • Spirituality of Existence

Selected Publications
